About Me

Hi there I am Nadia!

I have done all sorts of things, some of them are cool and some not so cool but overall they are mostly cool. I moved to the US- which I have done a number of times, as I was technically born here but I am not really from here. So yes, I moved to Chicago about 1.5 years ago from Pakistan to become a tech nerd. A brown lady tech nerd. I attended Dev Bootcamp where I became a Sea Lion (weird I know), and I learned how to code which was one of the hardest yet rewarding accomplishments of my life. I immersed myself in the Chicago Tech scene, you can always spot me at a meet up rocking a different pair of glasses each time. The lady tech community has been a huge support in my journey to becoming a software developer and giving back to it gives me immense joy- I love to TA at Railsbridge and DBC, if you ever need an extra pair of hands I am always willing to help out. I am a woman of many talents, not to brag, but I love painting, designing clothes, cooking the best noms, dancing like a pro, traveling (visited 13 or so countries), talking to people- all the people about everything, leading teams, and coming up with brilliant yet weird ideas. I am excited to continue my journey in tech and hope that I can eventually merge/mush my coding skills, people skills, and management experience. Okay, done sharing but if you want to know more about feel free to contact me!

Projects

TopThat is a mobile responsive web application that allows users to host videos and interact competitively within the TopThat social network. The app was built with Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, and AJAX, while integrating external technologies like Carrierwave, Amazon S3, Redis, and Sidekiq. Testing was conducted with RSpec, Shoulda Matchers, and Travis CI, and the app was deployed on Heroku


Overflow Your Boat is a light version of Stack Overflow with CRUD capabilities for creating questions, answers, and comments. The web application is built with Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, jQuery, and AJAX. It is tested using RSpec, Travis CI, and deployed on Heroku.
